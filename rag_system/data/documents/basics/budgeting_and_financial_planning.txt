Title: The Importance of Budgeting and Financial Planning

Budgeting means creating a plan for how you will spend and save your money. It helps you control your expenses and achieve financial goals.

A simple budget divides income into three parts: needs, wants, and savings. A good rule of thumb is the 50-30-20 rule:
- 50% for needs (rent, groceries, bills)
- 30% for wants (entertainment, travel)
- 20% for savings and investments

Financial planning means setting short-term and long-term goals such as buying a vehicle, education, or retirement.

An emergency fund should have at least 3 to 6 months of living expenses. It protects you from unexpected situations.

Avoid unnecessary debt, pay bills on time, and track spending using mobile apps or notebooks. Small, consistent savings lead to long-term wealth creation.

Teaching financial planning from an early age helps people make informed and responsible financial decisions.
